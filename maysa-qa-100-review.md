Swedish Relaxation Massage uses long, smooth strokes and gentle kneading focused purely on physical relaxation and circulation. There's no stretching or acupressure element. It's often the most comfortable choice for first-time spa guests.

In short: Thai Oil is more therapeutic and invigorating, Swedish is softer and more purely relaxing. Both come in 60 or 90 minutes (Thai Oil also has 120).

### 91. What is the difference between Thai massage and deep tissue?

Sawasdee kha, I'm Maysa from Ton Mai Spa. Lovely question — here's the honest short version.

Thai Traditional Massage is the ancient one: fully clothed, no oil, the therapist uses palms, thumbs, elbows and even feet to work along your energy lines, with lots of passive stretching. It feels rhythmic, opening, almost yoga-like. People describe it as energising.

Deep Tissue uses oil and firm, focused pressure on specific knots and chronic tension in the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. It's corrective work — slower, more targeted, and usually quieter on the table. People describe it as relieving.

If you want to relax and stretch, go Thai. If you have a stubborn knot or postural tension, go Deep Tissue.
