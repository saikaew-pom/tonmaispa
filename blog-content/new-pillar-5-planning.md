### Article

Booking policies are nobody's favorite reading, right up until the moment plans change and you desperately need to know where you stand. Holidays are unpredictable — a flight shifts, a day gets rained out, someone in the group decides they'd rather nap. So here, in plain language and all in one place, is exactly how cancellations, deposits and rescheduling work at Ton Mai Spa, so there are no unpleasant surprises in either direction.

#### Cancellation Policy

Here's the clear version:

- **More than 48 hours before your appointment:** fully refunded, no fuss.
- **Within 24 to 48 hours:** a 50% fee applies.
- **Less than 24 hours before:** non-refundable.

The logic is simply fairness: a therapist's time is booked and held for you, and a last-minute cancellation is a slot nobody else could take. The 48-hour window gives you plenty of room to change your mind freely, while the shorter windows reflect the reality that we've reserved that time specifically for you. **To cancel, just message us on WhatsApp** at +66 82 286 6058 — it's the fastest, clearest way, and you'll have it in writing.

#### Do You Need a Deposit?

For standard individual treatments, the honest answer is that booking is refreshingly simple: you submit a booking request, we confirm it, and you pay when you're here — there's no online pre-payment to wrestle with. For larger arrangements — bigger groups, or longer high-value combinations — we may ask to confirm details more firmly in advance, simply because they take more coordination. If anything like that applies to your booking, we'll tell you clearly at the time rather than springing it on you. When in doubt, a quick WhatsApp message gets you a straight answer for your specific booking.

#### Rescheduling: Easier Than Cancelling

Nine times out of ten, people who think they need to cancel actually just need to *move* their booking — and rescheduling is genuinely easy. If your plans shift, you don't have to lose your slot; you can move it.

There are two simple ways to do it:

1. **Through the chat on our website.** If you booked via our site, our chat assistant can pull up your booking (after a quick secure verification, so we know it's really you) and help you move it to a new time — you'll see a summary card, confirm the new slot, and it's done. Your reference number stays the same.
2. **On WhatsApp.** Message the team and we'll move it for you directly.

Either way, a rescheduled booking goes back to "pending" until our team reconfirms the new time — which is us double-checking a therapist is available at your new slot, not a hoop for you to jump through. For the whole booking process from scratch, our [how to book a spa day in Phuket guide](/blog/how-to-book-a-spa-day-phuket) walks through it start to finish.

#### What Happens After You Submit a Booking

A small but genuinely reassuring thing to understand: when you submit a booking, it arrives as a *request*, marked pending, and our team reviews and confirms it. So a fresh booking isn't instantly "locked" the second you hit submit — it's received, and then confirmed by a human, at which point you'll get a confirmation by email or WhatsApp. This is a feature, not a delay: it means a real person has checked the slot works before it's set in stone.

#### Payment

We accept **cash and credit card**. Nothing exotic required — pay when you're here, in whichever of those is easiest for you.

#### Changed Your Mind About the Treatment, Not the Time?

That's fine too, and it's common — people book a massage and then fancy a facial, or want to add the thermal circuit. As long as there's a therapist and room for whatever you're switching to, we'll sort it out; the mechanics of changes and add-ons are covered in our [groups, couples and special occasions guide](/blog/booking-groups-couples-occasions).

#### The Bottom Line

Cancel more than 48 hours ahead and it's fully refunded; inside that, fees step up as the appointment nears. No online deposit for standard treatments. Rescheduling is easy and keeps your reference. And every booking is confirmed by a real person before it's final. When anything's unclear, one WhatsApp message to +66 82 286 6058 settles it — and if you're still deciding whether to book ahead at all, our [walk-in or book ahead guide](/blog/walk-in-or-book-ahead-timing) is the companion read.

## Post 2: What to Bring and What to Expect on Your First Spa Visit

### Article

First-time spa nerves almost always come down to a fear of doing it "wrong" — showing up with the incorrect things, or without the correct ones, and being quietly judged by people in robes. Good news: there is very little to get wrong, and we've never once judged anyone. But a short, honest guide to what to bring and what actually happens does take the edge off, so here it is.

#### What to Bring: Almost Nothing

Genuinely, almost nothing. We provide the essentials:

- **Towels** — plenty of them, provided.
- **Robe** — provided, for wearing between and around treatments.
- **Flip-flops / sandals** — provided, so you're not padding around barefoot.
- **Lockers** — for stashing your things safely while you relax.

So the "what do I bring?" answer is, for a standard treatment, *just yourself*. Maybe your phone for a photo under the tree, and that's about it.

The one thing worth bringing yourself: **a swimsuit, if you plan to use the pool or the thermal circuit.** We can provide the robe and towels, but swimwear is a personal item — so if the pool or circuit is on your agenda, pack one. (First time trying the circuit? Our [thermal circuit walk-through](/blog/thermal-circuit-steam-sauna-cold-plunge-explained) explains exactly how it flows.)

#### What to Wear

Come in whatever's comfortable — you'll change into a robe for oil-based treatments, or stay in loose, comfortable clothing for a traditional Thai massage (which is done fully clothed). If you're coming for a [facial](/blog/facials-guide-signature-brightening-mens), arriving with a relatively clean, product-free face is a small kindness to your therapist, but not essential.

One gentle tip: leave valuables and lots of jewelry at your accommodation if you can. We have lockers, but the less you have to think about, the more you can actually switch off.

#### What to Expect When You Arrive

- **Arrive 15–20 minutes early.** Not out of strictness — it just gives you time for a brief consultation, changing, and a moment to settle so you're not walking in mid-adrenaline.
- **A short consultation.** We'll ask about pressure preference, any areas to focus on, and — importantly — any health considerations. Which brings us to the one thing that genuinely matters most.

#### The One Thing That Genuinely Matters: Speak Up

Tell us about any health considerations — pregnancy, injuries, high blood pressure, allergies, recent surgery, anything relevant. This isn't box-ticking; it changes which treatments are appropriate and how they're adapted. And during the treatment itself, keep speaking up: too much pressure, too little, too warm, too cold — say so. A good therapist checks in, but they're not telepathic, and there's no medal for silently enduring. Your comfort is the entire point.

#### A Few House Basics

- **Minimum age is 10** at Ton Mai, for both treatments and the thermal circuit.
- **No outside food or drinks**, please — it keeps the space calm and tidy, and our [garden café](/blog/garden-cafe-healthy-thai-food) has you covered if you're hungry.
- **Staying connected:** if you need Wi-Fi or anything practical during your visit, just ask the team when you arrive — we'd rather sort it in person than have you assume.

#### Still Not Sure What to Book?

Completely normal, and not a problem you need to solve before arriving. "I want to relax," "my shoulders are wrecked," or "surprise me with something authentically Thai" is plenty to work with. Our [choose the right treatment guide](/blog/how-to-choose-right-treatment-first-visit) helps if you'd like to arrive with a plan, but the front desk (and the chat assistant on our site) are happy to steer you.

#### The Bottom Line

Bring yourself, and a swimsuit if the pool's on the cards — we provide the rest. Arrive a little early, speak up about health and comfort, and let the rest happen. First visits are far less of a test than they feel from the outside; the correct way to do it is, genuinely, however feels good to you.

## Post 3: Walk-In or Book Ahead? Timing Your Spa Visit in Rawai

### Article

"Can I just turn up?" is one of the most common questions we get, and the honest answer is a satisfying *usually, yes — but here's when you really shouldn't chance it.* Phuket rewards spontaneity right up until it doesn't, and knowing the difference means you're never the person standing hopefully at reception during the one fully-booked hour of the week.

#### Walk-Ins: Genuinely Welcome

Let's start with the good news: **walk-ins are welcome at Ton Mai.** We're open every day from 9am to 11pm — a wide window — and plenty of guests wander in on a whim and get seen. The **thermal circuit in particular needs no reservation at all**: the day pass is walk-in by design, so you can drop in for steam, sauna, cold plunge and pool any time we're open, no planning required.

So if you're the spontaneous type, you're not shut out. Turn up, and there's a good chance we'll sort you out.

#### When to Book Ahead (Please)

That said, there are moments when "just turning up" is a gamble you'll sometimes lose:

- **Weekends and public holidays.** Busier by nature — some slots fill early. Book ahead.
- **Peak season** (roughly December to February, and around the mid-April Songkran holiday). The island is fuller, and so are we. Our [best time of year to visit guide](/blog/best-time-to-visit-rawai-nai-harn) explains the seasonal rhythm.
- **Specific times you're set on** — sunset, a particular gap in your day, an evening slot.
- **Longer combinations** — a massage plus the full thermal circuit, or anything that needs more scheduling room.
- **Groups.** For 3 or more people, please message us at least 24 hours ahead so we can line up therapists and rooms — walk-in group visits are the one thing that genuinely tends not to work.

Booking ahead for these isn't bureaucracy; it's the difference between a smooth afternoon and a disappointed one.

#### Same-Day Bookings

Somewhere between "walk in cold" and "book a week out" is the sweet spot most travelers actually use: a **same-day message.** A quick WhatsApp in the morning — "any space for a 90-minute massage this afternoon?" — gets you a real answer and holds your slot, without committing days ahead. It's the fastest way to lock in a same-day visit, and the [ways to reach us are all here](/blog/opening-hours-and-contact).

#### Running Late?

It happens — Phuket traffic, a long lunch, island time in general. If you're running behind for a booked treatment, **message us as soon as you know.** We'll always do our best to hold your time or adjust, but a treatment is a booked slot with a therapist waiting, so a heads-up gives everyone the best shot at still making it work. Silence is the only thing that really hurts your chances.

#### What If Your Preferred Time Is Full?

If the exact slot you wanted is taken, it's rarely a dead end. There's usually a nearby time that works, a slightly different treatment that's free, or — very often — the thermal circuit to enjoy while a slot opens up, since that needs no booking. Just ask; we'd much rather find you an alternative than send you away. And once you've picked a time, our [booking policies guide](/blog/booking-policies-cancellation-deposit-reschedule) covers what happens next.

#### The Bottom Line

Walk in freely for the thermal circuit and for quiet weekday hours. Book ahead for weekends, holidays, peak season, evenings, groups, and any time you're set on a specific slot. And when in doubt, a same-day WhatsApp is the traveler's cheat code — spontaneous enough to suit a holiday, planned enough to actually get you in. For the full first-timer booking walkthrough, [how to book a spa day in Phuket](/blog/how-to-book-a-spa-day-phuket) has it all.

### Article

A spa visit is lovely on your own; it's a genuinely special shared experience with the right person, or the right people. Anniversaries, honeymoons, a friends' trip, a "we survived the family holiday, we've earned this" afternoon — some of the best visits to Ton Mai involve more than one set of feet up. Here's how to make the booking side of that smooth, so the day itself is all garden and calm and none of the "wait, are we actually all booked?"

#### Couples

For two, our **Couple's Bliss Package** is built for exactly this: side-by-side in our couple's room, the thermal circuit together, a 90-minute Thai oil massage each, and a jasmine foot soak — about three hours, and a genuine anniversary-grade treat at **4,800 THB for both of you.** It's the easy answer when you want the shared experience done properly, with the two of you in the same room rather than politely relaxing in separate ones.

If you'd rather build your own pairing, that's fine too — two individual treatments booked for the same time works beautifully. For a fuller look at the romantic options, including gift ideas, our [best couples spa packages guide](/blog/best-couples-spa-packages-phuket) is the one to read.

#### Groups

For **3 or more people, please message us at least 24 hours ahead.** This is the single most important logistical thing in this whole post. A group needs multiple therapists free at the same time and enough treatment rooms, and that genuinely takes coordination — it's the one scenario where walking in and hoping tends not to work. Give us a day's notice on WhatsApp, tell us the group size and roughly what everyone's after, and we'll build a plan that actually fits, rather than everyone scrambling at check-in.

Groups also open up nice options: some people having treatments while others enjoy the thermal circuit and the garden café, staggered timings so nobody's waiting around, that sort of thing. The more we know up front, the better we can choreograph it.

#### Everyone Wanting Something Different

Totally normal, and totally fine. One person wants a deep tissue, another a facial, someone just wants the thermal circuit and a smoothie — we can absolutely mix that. The trick is telling us the full picture when you book, so we can line up the right therapists for the right treatments at compatible times. Add-ons can be part of the mix too — our [add-ons guide](/blog/massage-add-ons-explained) covers the little extras, and yes, you can usually add one on even after booking.

#### Can I Request a Specific Therapist?

You can mention a preference, and we'll do our best to accommodate it — but we can't cast-iron guarantee a specific therapist for a specific slot, because their schedules genuinely fill up. The honest approach: tell us who or what you'd like when you book, and we'll make it happen if we can. What we *won't* do is quietly promise something we're not sure we can deliver.

#### Changing or Adding After You've Booked

Plans within plans shift, especially for groups — someone drops out, someone wants to add a treatment, the timing needs a nudge. All manageable: message us and we'll adjust where we can. The mechanics of changes, cancellations and what happens after you submit are all in our [booking policies guide](/blog/booking-policies-cancellation-deposit-reschedule) — worth a skim before a group booking so everyone knows the lay of the land.

#### The Bottom Line

Couples: the Couple's Bliss Package is the easy, lovely answer, or build your own pair. Groups of 3+: message us 24 hours ahead, every time — that one habit makes group visits work. Mixed wishes and specific requests: tell us everything up front and we'll choreograph it. Do that, and a shared spa afternoon becomes one of the genuinely memorable bits of the trip, rather than a booking headache. For the value angle across it all, [day pass vs. single treatment](/blog/day-pass-vs-single-treatment-value) is a useful companion.
