### Article

Most spas are built and then named. Ours happened the other way around: the name came first, because the reason the whole place exists was already standing on the land, roots down, when everything else was just an idea.

#### A Tree Worth Saving

Ton Mai Spa was founded by Hervé, who came across a plot of land in Rawai with a 150-year-old tamarind tree growing at its heart. The straightforward, profitable thing to do with a plot like that is to clear it — trees are, from a developer's point of view, an inconvenience with deep roots. Hervé looked at this particular 150-year-old inconvenience and decided the opposite: he bought the land specifically to *save* the tree, and then built the spa around it rather than in place of it.

"Ton Mai" means "old tree" in Thai. It's not a poetic flourish invented by a marketing team; it's simply the truth of the place, named plainly. Every guest who comes to Ton Mai walks under that tree on their way in. It's the first thing that greets you and, on the way out, the last — a living, 150-year-old reminder of why any of this is here.

#### Why It Shapes Everything

A founding story like that isn't just a nice anecdote for the "about" page; it genuinely sets the tone for how the whole spa works. A place that started by refusing to bulldoze a tree tends to make a lot of small decisions the same way — choosing the calmer, more rooted option over the shinier, faster one.

That's why Ton Mai isn't a marble-and-chrome, luxury-for-its-own-sake sort of spa. It's a garden. Teak wood, woven rattan, terracotta, herbal tea in wicker baskets, treatment salas connected by shaded garden paths rather than corridors. The whole design philosophy is "grown, not built" — which is also, not coincidentally, how we think about wellness itself. It's the same instinct behind [why we built a full wellness circuit](/blog/why-we-built-a-full-wellness-circuit) instead of just a massage menu, and behind our [garden-to-table approach in the restaurant](/blog/garden-to-table-ingredient-sourcing): do the slower, more honest version of the thing.

#### Renewal, Not Luxury

There's a meaningful difference between a spa that sells luxury and a spa that offers renewal, even if the two can look similar from the outside. Luxury is about how expensive the experience feels. Renewal is about how you feel *after* — lighter, clearer, more yourself. A 150-year-old tree doesn't survive a century and a half by being flashy; it survives by being deeply rooted and quietly resilient. That's the feeling we're actually chasing for guests, and it's why "come for an hour or stay the afternoon" is a genuine invitation rather than an upsell.

#### Come Walk Under It

You don't need to book a treatment to appreciate the tree — though we'd love it if you did. You're welcome to come, walk in under that broad old canopy, have something at [the garden café](/blog/garden-cafe-healthy-thai-food), and simply feel the difference a place built around a tree feels like. If you'd like to fold it into a fuller day, our [half-day in Rawai itinerary](/blog/half-day-rawai-beach-spa-itinerary) — beach in the morning, spa in the afternoon — is the classic template, and the tree makes a lovely place to end up.

#### The Bottom Line

Ton Mai Spa exists because someone decided a 150-year-old tree was worth more than the land it stood on. Everything else — the garden design, the unhurried treatments, the herbal tea in wicker baskets — grew out of that one decision. Come stand under the tree once and the whole place makes a bit more sense.

### Article

Rawai sits right down at the southern tip of Phuket, which is either a small journey or a lovely excuse for a scenic drive, depending on your mood and where you're staying. The good news is that it's genuinely easy to reach from anywhere on the island, and once you know the rough distances, planning your visit to Ton Mai Spa becomes a five-second decision rather than a logistics puzzle.

Here's the practical map.

#### Where We Are

Ton Mai Spa is at **6/11 Moo 2 Wiset Road, Rawai, Phuket 83130** — on Wiset Road, the main artery running through Rawai and up toward Nai Harn. Here's the direct map link for whichever app you use: [Google Maps directions to Ton Mai Spa](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&destination=Ton+Mai+Spa+Rawai+Phuket). Punch that in and the navigation takes care of the rest.

#### Rough Travel Times

These are approximate and depend on traffic, but they'll get you planning:

- **From Nai Harn Beach:** about **5 minutes** — we're practically neighbors, which is why "beach morning, spa afternoon" is such an easy combination.
- **From Kata or Karon:** about **15 minutes** north up the coast.
- **From Chalong:** roughly **10 minutes**.
- **From Patong:** about **40 minutes** by taxi — a proper little drive, but a scenic one, and very doable as a half-day outing.
- **From Phuket International Airport:** allow around **an hour**, sometimes a little more with traffic — Rawai is about as far from the airport as you can get on the island, being right at the southern end.

If you're weighing up whether to base yourself in the buzzy north or the calmer south of the island in the first place, our [Rawai vs. Patong comparison](/blog/rawai-vs-patong-phuket-comparison) is a genuinely useful read.

#### Getting Here: Your Options

- **Taxi or ride-hailing app.** The simplest option, especially from further afield like Patong or the airport. Apps like Grab work in Phuket and take the haggling out of it; metered and negotiated taxis are also plentiful. Just show the driver the map pin.
- **Scooter.** If you're comfortable on two wheels and already renting one, Rawai is easy to reach and we have parking (more below). Wiset Road is straightforward to follow.
- **Your own car.** Also easy — see parking, next.

#### Parking

Simple: **we have free parking on site.** No hunting for a space, no fees, no drama. Turn up by car or scooter and there's room for you. This is one of the quiet perks of a garden spa in Rawai rather than a hotel spa in a congested resort strip — space isn't at a premium here.

#### A Note on Timing Your Arrival

We're open every day from **9am to 11pm**, which gives you a wide window and means you can dodge the worst of the midday heat and traffic if you like. If you're coming a fair distance — from Patong or the airport, say — it's worth a quick message ahead so we know to expect you, especially for a booked treatment; our [opening hours and contact guide](/blog/opening-hours-and-contact) has all the ways to reach us. For the finer detail on the immediate approach — nearby landmarks, exactly where to turn — our older [directions and parking guide](/blog/getting-to-ton-mai-spa-directions) zooms right in.

#### The Bottom Line

Rawai is at Phuket's southern tip: 5 minutes from Nai Harn, 15 from Kata, and about 40 by taxi from Patong. Grab a taxi, ride a scooter, or drive — and either way, park free on site when you arrive. Once you're here, the hardest part of the day is officially over. If you want to make a proper outing of it, our [half-day in Rawai itinerary](/blog/half-day-rawai-beach-spa-itinerary) shows you how to pair us with the beach.
